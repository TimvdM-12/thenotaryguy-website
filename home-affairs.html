Home Affairs Document Services

We help you navigate the Department of Home Affairs process and ensure your documents are ready for use locally and abroad.

The Department of Home Affairs (DHA) issues essential civil status documents that South Africans need throughout their lives. From birth certificates to marriage certificates and death certificates, these documents often need to be authenticated or apostilled for use in legal proceedings, immigration applications, or international transactions. The Notary Guy provides comprehensive assistance with DHA documents, helping you obtain, authenticate, and legalise these critical records.

Whether you need a birth certificate apostilled for a foreign visa application, a marriage certificate authenticated for international use, or assistance with a new DHA application, our team handles the process efficiently and keeps you informed at every step via WhatsApp.

Document Types We Assist With

Birth Certificates

Unabridged birth certificates for visa applications, immigration, and international use. We can assist with obtaining new certificates and apostilling existing ones.

Marriage Certificates

Marriage certificate authentication and apostille for use abroad. Required for spousal visa applications, name changes, and legal proceedings in foreign countries.

Death Certificates

Death certificate legalisation for international estate matters, insurance claims, and administrative purposes in foreign jurisdictions.

ID & Passport

Assistance with ID and passport applications, as well as certified copies and notarisation of identity documents for various legal purposes.

Our DHA Assistance Services

  • Application assistance — We guide you through the DHA application process, helping you complete forms correctly and gather the required supporting documents.
  • Document apostille — Once you have your DHA documents, we handle the High Court apostille process so they are accepted internationally under the Hague Convention.
  • Document authentication — For countries not part of the Hague Convention, we arrange full authentication and legalisation through DIRCO and the relevant embassy.
  • Notarised certified copies — We provide notarially certified copies of your DHA documents where required by banks, embassies, or foreign authorities.

Common Scenarios

Many South Africans require DHA document assistance when emigrating, applying for dual citizenship, getting married abroad, enrolling children in international schools, or settling estates with international components. In each case, the foreign authority will typically require your South African DHA documents to be apostilled or authenticated. The Notary Guy streamlines this process so you can focus on your plans while we handle the paperwork.
