Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about our notary services in South Africa.

What is a notary public and what do they do?

A notary public is a legal professional authorised to perform certain legal formalities, especially witnessing signatures on documents, administering oaths, and certifying copies of documents. In South Africa, a notary must be an admitted attorney who has passed additional examinations.

How much does it cost to notarise a document?

Document notarisation starts from R300 per document. The exact cost may vary depending on the complexity and type of document. Contact us for a detailed quote tailored to your specific needs.

What is an apostille and when do I need one?

An apostille is a certificate issued by the High Court that authenticates a document for use in countries that are members of the Hague Apostille Convention. You need an apostille when submitting South African documents to foreign authorities, such as for immigration, education, or business purposes abroad.

What is an antenuptial contract (ANC)?

An antenuptial contract is a legal agreement entered into before marriage that determines the matrimonial property regime of the marriage. It can be with or without the accrual system and must be signed before a notary and registered at the Deeds Office before the marriage takes place.

Can I upload my documents online for inspection?

Yes! You can use our Upload Documents section to submit your scanned documents. After filling in your details, you'll be connected to our notary via WhatsApp where you can send your documents for pre-inspection. We'll review them and guide you on the next steps.

How long does the notarisation process take?

Simple notarisations can often be completed within 24-48 hours. More complex matters such as apostilles and authentications may take longer depending on the requirements. Contact us for an estimated timeline for your specific needs.
